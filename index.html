The Incubator Roadmap

1

Weeks 1-3: The Friction Hunt (Design Thinking)

Goal: Identify a problem worth solving.

  • Activity: "User Interviews" with School Ops staff (Cafeteria, Reception, Nurse, Bus Drivers).
  • Output: A "Problem Board" identifying time-wasting tasks.
  • Key Question: "What is the most boring thing you do every day?"
2

Weeks 4-8: The No-Code Build (Prototyping)

Goal: Build a "Minimum Viable Product" (MVP).

  • Curriculum: Crash course in Prompt Engineering and RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation).
  • Workshops: "Building your first Brain with Chipp.AI" and "Making it Mobile with Glide."
  • Milestone: A working app on a student's phone by Week 6 that solves the identified problem.
3

Weeks 9-10: Demo Day (Launch)

Goal: Sell the solution to the stakeholders.

  • Event: A Shark Tank-style presentation to School Leadership.
  • Deliverable: Not just code, but a "User Manual" and a "Value Pitch" (e.g., Time Saved).
  • Prize: Official adoption of the tool by the school.

Safety & Ethics Protocol

The "Public Data Only" Rule

Strict Adherence Required: To protect student privacy, this incubator operates under a "Zero Trust" model regarding Personally Identifiable Information (PII).

Students are strictly forbidden from uploading:

  • Student Rosters with full names.
  • Grades or Transcripts.
  • Medical or Behavioral records.
  • Parent email lists (unless dummy data is used).

Violation of this rule results in immediate removal from the program.


The Student Pledge

Before writing a single prompt, every student signs this agreement:

"I promise to use AI to build, not to deceive. I will never upload private data about my classmates or teachers. I will always disclose that my tool is AI-powered. I am an architect of the future, and I build with integrity."
